About

The Missing Links is an archaeology of digital journalism, exploring the ways that news becomes history, as the web unfolds in real time and unfurls into the past. It traces news archives (both physical and digital), hyperlinks, and organization systems.

It started life as a masters thesis in MIT's Comparative Media Studies program, advised by William Uricchio, Kurt Fendt, and Ethan Zuckerman. Copyright Massachusetts Institute of Technology, 2015.

This site primarily consists of writings and arguments from the thesis, but also doubles as a meta-exploration of how this digital container reframes and enriches the arguments within. It allows the reader to dive in by chronology, tag, or link in addition to reading page by page.
